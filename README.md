## Rough Title

North Atlantic Hurricane Activity and Storm Intensity Since 1970

## Research Question

How has North Atlantic hurricane activity, storm intensity, and storm-track behavior changed since 1970, and which years stand out as unusually active or intense?

## Dataset

This project will use the NOAA IBTrACS hurricane dataset.

Dataset link:

https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/data/international-best-track-archive-for-climate-stewardship-ibtracs/v04r00/access/csv/ibtracs.ALL.list.v04r00.csv

Dataset source:

https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/products/international-best-track-archive

## Planned Analysis Summary

We will use the NOAA IBTrACS hurricane track dataset to analyze North Atlantic storms since 1970.

Our analysis will use Pandas to filter the dataset, group storms by year and storm ID, calculate yearly storm counts, identify the strongest storms by wind speed and pressure, and examine seasonal and spatial patterns in hurricane activity.

We plan to visualize the results with time series plots, bar charts of the strongest storms, storm track maps, spatial density plots, and seasonal climatology figures.

## Division of Work

Jiaqi Ma will analyze North Atlantic hurricane activity over time, including annual storm counts, daily datapoint counts, seasonal climatology, and anomalous years.

Tianhua Wang will analyze hurricane intensity, including maximum wind speed, minimum pressure, strongest storms, and changes in intensity over time.

Boyang Liu will analyze spatial patterns of hurricane tracks, including storm track maps, geographic density, and comparisons across selected active years.
